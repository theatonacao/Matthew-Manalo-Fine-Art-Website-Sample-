F R E Q U E N T L Y     A S K E D     Q U E S T I O N S

Q: What do you specialize in?

A: I specialize in photorealism and hyperrealism

Q: What are photorealism and hyperrealism? What's the difference between them?

A: Photorealism is a modern genre of painting where the subject is in a photo instead of having the conventional live subject. Hyperrealism is a more refined and advanced form of photorealism wherein the painting is so realistic or photo-like that they are almost indistinguishable from the photo, typically HD looking.

Q: What is portraiture?

A: The art of making portraits.

Q: What is a portrait?

A: A portrait focuses on the human face as a subject. It may have different forms like a sculpture, photograph, painting and etc.

Q: Do you offer lessons?

A: Yes, it's a 9 hour, 3-day course where we give you the fundamental skills in making a photorealistic portrait, techniques that professionals use.

Q: Can I really make a portrait when I have no background or practice in art?

A: Yes, Matthew used to teach at Macky Bongabong Fine Art where they've had over 400 students and not one was dissatisfied nor failed to create a decent portrait. The teaching techniques have been developed, practiced and proven.

Q: Why ask someone to paint a photorealistic portrait over printing an actual photo then framing it?

A: The difference is the value.
